## Summary

The SpinPhase GW150914 blind detection test ranked the GW150914 segment **52nd out of 100** by the pre-registered Z metric. Per the pre-registered decision criteria (Section 6.2), this falls in the **R ≥ 11** band: *"No detection capability under this test."*

The framework, in this configuration (fixed GW150914-morphology chirp track, cross-detector phase coherence, 50-shift cyclic null distribution, ±10 ms Δt search), does not distinguish the GW150914 segment from background under this protocol. The hypothesis registered for v1 is falsified.

## Diagnostic Reading

The Z distribution across the 100 segments has the broad characteristics expected of noise: a roughly symmetric spread around zero, with a positive upper tail extending to Z ≈ +3.3 and a negative lower tail extending to Z ≈ −2.5. This is the shape of a sample of 100 standardized scores under the null hypothesis that all segments are statistically equivalent.

The top-of-distribution segments (Z = +3.31, +2.93, +2.59) appear to be ordinary statistical fluctuations in noise. Their M_obs values (0.65–0.75) are elevated above their μ_null values, but this is exactly the behavior expected from the upper tail of 100 samples drawn from the framework's noise distribution. None of these segments correspond to known GW events; they are background segments where the random phase alignment along the chirp track happened to be coherent.

The GW150914 segment's score, embedded in the middle of this distribution, provides no indication that the framework's response distinguishes signal from noise. Whatever structural feature SpinPhase measures, it is not preferentially activated by the GW150914 inspiral signal as expressed in the H1/L1 strain.

## Methodological Observations

Two implementation observations are noted as part of the locked record. Neither alters the primary outcome but each is relevant for v2 design or external review.

### Metadata leak via GPS times in the blinded file

`segments_blinded.csv` contains the schema (`blinded_segment_id`, `h1_start_gps`, `l1_start_gps`) per pre-registration Section 10.1. The GPS times are needed to fetch strain. However, the GW150914 segment's start GPS (1126259446.4 = published merger time 1126259462.4 minus 16 seconds) is publicly known, so the GW150914 row can be identified from `segments_blinded.csv` without the sealed key.

This was noticed during inspection of the score output, after scoring was complete and `scores.csv` was committed. It did not affect the test outcome because:

- The pipeline code was frozen and committed at `335ce7b` before any scoring ran.
- The pipeline reads strain only; it does not read GPS times during scoring.
- The scoring is fully deterministic given strain inputs.
- No analyst intervention could have influenced any segment's Z value.

The pre-registration acknowledges this class of limitation in Section 10.1: *"If the analyst conducting scoring is the same person who generated the segments, the integrity of the blinding depends on this person's discipline."* The freeze commit operationally enforced what the metadata-level blinding only nominally required.

A v2 design should close this hole by storing only blinded GPS offsets in the committed file and unsealing the offsets at the same time as the segment-type mapping.

### Sub-bin Δt implementation via frequency-domain phase rotation

Pre-registration Section 4.4 specifies `X_L1(t_k − Δt, f_k)` as a literal time-shifted STFT lookup. The Δt search range (±10 ms) is finer than the STFT time-bin spacing (~31 ms), making a literal bin lookup degenerate.

The implementation at `analysis/spinphase_gw_blind/spinphase_pipeline.py` (committed at `335ce7b`) realizes the Δt shift via frequency-domain phase rotation: `X_L1(t_k − Δt, f_k) ≡ X_L1(t_k, f_k) · exp(−2π i f_k Δt)`. This is the standard approach to sub-bin time shifts in GW phase-coherence analyses and is the only mathematically meaningful reading of Section 4.4 given the search granularity. The pipeline docstring documents this choice in detail.

A v2 may either fix the Δt search step at the STFT bin spacing (eliminating the sub-bin issue) or pre-register the phase-rotation implementation explicitly.

## What This Falsifies, and What It Does Not

### Falsified

- The hypothesis that SpinPhase, configured with the v1 protocol — fixed GW150914-morphology chirp track, cross-detector phase coherence with ±10 ms Δt search, 50-shift cyclic null distribution, single Welch-PSD whitening per segment — can rank the GW150914 segment near the top of a 100-segment population including 99 PSD-matched O1 background segments.

The framework as configured does not distinguish the GW150914 segment from background.

### Not Falsified

- That SpinPhase, with a different parameterization (chirp-track grid, expanded Δt search, larger n_null), might show different behavior. This is a v2 hypothesis and would require its own pre-registration.
- That the broader CI/CSO/MCC research program has scientific merit at some configuration not yet tested.
- The Dynametrix weather pre-registrations (v1, v2, v3, v3a) are independent of this result. They concern a different framework configuration applied to atmospheric data and are bound by their own separate pre-registrations and outcomes.

### Implications for v2

A `spinphase-gw-blind-v2` pre-registration, if designed, would address at least the following items observed during v1:

- **Blinded GPS metadata.** Store random GPS offsets in the blinded population file; unseal both the segment-type mapping and the offset-to-real-GPS mapping at the unblinding moment.
- **Larger null distribution.** Expand n_null from 50 to ≥ 500 to enable formal p-value estimation alongside ranking.
- **Chirp-track parameterization.** Pre-register a chirp-track grid with appropriate trial-factor correction, removing the GW150914-morphology specificity of v1.
- **Multi-event extension.** Test against additional confirmed O1 / O2 / O3 events rather than the single-event ranking of v1.
- **Sub-bin Δt search semantics.** Pre-register the phase-rotation implementation explicitly, or fix the search step at the STFT bin width.

v2 design does not invalidate the v1 result. v1 stands as the locked historical outcome of the v1 protocol.
